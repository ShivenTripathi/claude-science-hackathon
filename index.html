Selective Th2 Suppressor Re-analysis

A genome-scale CRISPRi Perturb-seq re-analysis in primary human CD4+ T cells — hunting knockdowns that suppress the Th2 (allergy) program without flipping cells toward Th1, stress-testing that claim to destruction, then asking whether a different, degrader-relevant phenotype is the more useful question.

Honest bottom line. On the core claim — selective Th2 suppression — the answer is a calibrated negative, reached independently by three tracks: competitive scoring collapses the arm-correlation confound (+0.54→+0.18), a permutation null shows no enrichment over random gene sets (FDR ≈ 1), and GATA3 reads as a Th1-skewer under the matched Th1-vs-Th0 arm. A fourth track asks a different question — not "selective," but "phenocopies the STAT6/GATA3 collapse," the bar an oral STAT6 degrader actually clears. That reframe calibrates as a classifier (anchors self-recover, permutation p < 0.001) and, fused with Open Targets tractability + AD genetics + AD patient skin, nominates druggable off-axis nodes (ITK, INPP5D/SHIP1) — a nomination map, not a validated target list, and it does not rescue the selectivity negative. See RECONCILIATION.md.
