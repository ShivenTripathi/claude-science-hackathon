Selective Th2 Suppressor Re-analysis

A genome-scale CRISPRi Perturb-seq re-analysis in primary human CD4+ T cells — hunting knockdowns that suppress the Th2 (allergy) program without flipping cells toward Th1, then stress-testing that claim to destruction.

Honest bottom line. Under rigorous statistics the naive selective-suppressor atlas does not hold up: competitive scoring collapses the arm-correlation confound (+0.54→+0.18), a permutation null shows no enrichment over random gene sets (FDR ≈ 1), and GATA3 reads as a Th1-skewer. This documents a calibrated negative result plus a reproducible pipeline and an agentic review layer — not a target list.
